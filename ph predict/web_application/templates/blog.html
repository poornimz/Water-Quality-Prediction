Water quality is measured by testing or monitoring chemical, physical, and biological properties. Some frequently monitored parameters include: Physical: Temperature, turbidity, solids, conductivity, salinity, color, taste, and odor Chemical: pH, ORP, acidity, alkalinity, chlorine, hardness, dissolved oxygen, and biological oxygen demand Biological: Nutrients, bacteria, algae, and viruses
