  • quality analysis

    Generally water quality assessment involves analysis of some physico-chemical, biological and microbiological parameters and reflects a-biotic and biotic status of the ecosystem. Such an assessment, in turn, helps in planning, utilization and antipollution and conversation strategies for their sustainable use. Among different parameters analyzed in the water quality assessment, the primary water quality criteria is based on 4 to 5 important ones, these are pH value, electrical conductivity (EC), dissolved oxygen (DO), chemical oxygen demand (COD) and total coliform (MPN). Nevertheless, other parameters are also important as indicators of water quality.

WHO produces international norms on water quality and human health in the form of guidelines that are used as the basis for regulation and standard setting world-wide. The Guidelines for drinking-water quality (GDWQ) promote the protection of public health by advocating for the development of locally relevant standards and regulations (health based targets), adoption of preventive risk management approaches covering catchment to consumer (Water Safety Plans) and independent surveillance to ensure that Water Safety Plans are being implemented and effective and that national standards are being met.
